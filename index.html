mLeftwich

My name is Matt Leftwich, I'm a developing web developer. I started my journey with web development way back in 2003 or so, at age 13. What started initially as a desire to create interesting functionality in the web based chat client mIRC turned into an all round passion for code and development.


Beginning on mIRC turned out to be quite fortuitous, as I was exposed to like minded people that shared a similar interest in web design and was able to continue my self-taught practice with the support of knowledgable and passionate individuals.


I carry this same drive and focus with me today as I embark on a new chapter along my development odyssey; an invigorating new challenge in the guise of a Web Development Bootcamp through Trilogy Education and the University of Western Australia, where I aim to broaden my skillset and hopefully build a life around something that I've always been keenly interested in.


The internet is an ever evolving machine, and as such developers need to be on the cusp of the relevant technologies used to interact within it. This will be my home to learn, share and experiment with the inspiring, and exciting possibilities available online today.

Inspired Eats

This is the result of the first big project I undertook during Bootcamp. Also the first time implementing what I had learned through the course, in a team of 3 aspiring developers. It was a great way to make use of all the knowledge we had gained and a practical way to show the different technologies/languages covered throughout the start of the course. We gained a good understanding of HTML, CSS, JavaScript and the use of API's.


The application is designed to generate a random recipe based upon the users location and the relevant weather conditions in their city. When the user first visits the website they are presented with a title, a random light-hearted inspiring quote as "food for thought", and a text entry field where they can enter their location, then click on a button to generate the result. The application generates an icon and provides the current temperature in that area, and then produces a recipe dependant on the conditions, i.e. hot or cold.


I enjoyed working with my co-developers Sara and Navneet on this project, we have discussed further development for the application such as the ability to save favourite recipes, a more comprehensive library for results, and more food-specific quotes. You can view the deployed application here and take the guesswork out of cooking today!.
